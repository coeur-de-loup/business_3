## Executive Summary

**Pricing Philosophy:** Transparent, predictable, and aligned with customer value. We charge for outcomes (workflows automated, tools consolidated), not usage (tasks, API calls).

**Core Pricing Strategy:**
- **Starter:** $49/month (1-5 employees, core features)
- **Professional:** $99/month (5-50 employees, advanced features)
- **Enterprise:** Custom pricing (50+ employees, full-service)

**Competitive Positioning:** 50-80% cheaper than enterprise tools, 20-40% more expensive than basic DIY tools, but with hands-on support and templates.

**Expected Mix:** 60% Starter, 35% Professional, 5% Enterprise = $75 ARPC

## 2. Pricing Strategy Rationale

### Why This Pricing Structure?

**1. Tiered by Business Size, Not Usage:**
- Problem: Usage-based pricing (Zapier) creates bill shock
- Solution: Flat monthly pricing based on team size
- Benefit: Predictable costs, no surprises

**2. Value-Based Pricing:**
- Competitors: Charge for tasks, API calls, seats
- Us: Charge for outcomes (workflows automated, tools consolidated)
- Evidence: Customers already paying $300-500/month for fragmented tools
- Our price: $49-99 (80-90% savings)

**3. Psychological Pricing:**
- $49 (not $50): Feels like "under $50"
- $99 (not $100): Just under psychological barrier
- Both end in "9": Classic pricing psychology

**4. Anchoring Effect:**
- Enterprise at $299 makes Professional at $99 seem like a bargain
- Most customers choose middle tier (decoy effect)
- Expected mix: 60% Starter, 35% Professional, 5% Enterprise

**5. Transparent Pricing:**
- No "contact sales for pricing"
- No hidden fees or usage limits (within tier)
- No per-user pricing (team pricing)
- Display pricing prominently on website

### Competitive Pricing Analysis

| Competitor | Pricing Model | Monthly Cost | Our Advantage |
|------------|--------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Zapier** | Usage-based (tasks) | $20-500+ | We're 50-90% cheaper, predictable |
| **Make** | Usage-based (operations) | $10-299 | We're 40-80% cheaper, better support |
| **n8n** | Self-hosted (free) or cloud ($20-140) | $20-140 | We're all-in-one, no coding required |
| **FlowiseAI** | Self-hosted (free) or cloud | $0-50 | We include templates and support |
| **Enterprise AI tools** | Per-user or per-seat | $100-500+ | We're 70-90% cheaper, SMB-focused |

**Our Sweet Spot:**
- Too expensive for DIYers ($20-30/month tools)
- Too cheap for enterprises ($200+/month tools)
- **Perfect for SMBs ($49-99/month)**

## 3. Monetization Experiments & Optimization

### Initial Pricing (Months 1-6): Validation Phase

**Goal:** Confirm willingness to pay at target price points

**Experiment 1: Price Anchoring**
- Show all 3 tiers on pricing page
- Highlight Professional tier as "Most Popular"
- Add "Limited time: 20% off for life" for first 100 customers
- Expected conversion: 60% Starter, 30% Professional, 10% Enterprise

**Experiment 2: Annual Discount**
- Offer 20% discount for annual payment
- $49/month → $39.20/month ($470/year)
- $99/month → $79.20/month ($950/year)
- Goal: 30-40% of customers choose annual (improves cash flow, reduces churn)

**Experiment 3: Free Trial vs. Freemium**
- **Option A:** 14-day free trial, credit card required
- **Option B:** Free tier forever (limited to 3 workflows, 1 integration)
- Test both, measure conversion and engagement
- Hypothesis: Free trial converts better (20-30% vs. 5-10% freemium)

### Pricing Optimization (Months 6-12): Data-Driven Adjustments

**Metric 1: Trial-to-Paid Conversion**
- Target: 20-30% conversion
- If <15%: Price too high or product not valuable enough
- If >40%: Price too low, leaving money on table

**Metric 2: Tier Selection**
- Expected: 60% Starter, 35% Professional, 5% Enterprise
- If 80%+ Starter: Underpriced Professional tier, add more features
- If 50%+ Enterprise: Underpriced Enterprise, raise base price

**Metric 3: Churn by Tier**
- Target: <5% monthly churn across all tiers
- If Starter churn >10%: Not enough value, add features
- If Enterprise churn >10%: Support issues, improve CSM

**Metric 4: Price Objections**
- Track sales calls and support tickets
- If >50% mention price: Test lower tier ($29/month)
- If <20% mention price: Room to raise prices 10-20%

### Pricing Experiments to Test

**Experiment 4: Lower Entry Tier**
- Add "Micro" tier at $29/month
- 3 workflows, 2 integrations, 1 user
- Target: Very small businesses (1-3 employees)
- Risk: Cannibalizes Starter tier

**Experiment 5: Higher Enterprise Tier**
- Raise Enterprise base from $299 to $499
- Add more features (custom integrations, training)
- Target: Mid-market companies (100-500 employees)
- Opportunity: Increase ARPC

**Experiment 6: Usage-Based Overage**
- Charge $0.01 per workflow run over limit
- Starter: 1,000 runs included, then $0.01/run
- Professional: 10,000 runs included, then $0.01/run
- Benefit: Monetize heavy users without tier upgrades

**Experiment 7: Add-On Pricing**
- Offer add-ons instead of tier upgrades
- +$20/month for 5 extra workflows
- +$30/month for custom integrations
- +$50/month for priority support
- Benefit: More granular pricing, higher ARPC

## 4. Discount & Promotional Strategy

### Launch Promotions (Months 1-3)

**Promotion 1: Founding Member Discount**
- **Offer:** 20% off for life
- **Limit:** First 100 customers
- **Goal:** Build initial customer base, generate testimonials
- **Expected Uptake:** 30-40% of early customers
- **Long-Term Impact:** Reduces ARPC by 8-12% (acceptable for early traction)

**Promotion 2: Annual Bonus**
- **Offer:** Buy annual, get 2 months free (25% discount)
- **Limit:** Unlimited
- **Goal:** Improve cash flow, reduce churn (annual customers stick longer)
- **Expected Uptake:** 20-30% of customers
- **Long-Term Impact:** Reduces churn by 30-50% for annual customers

**Promotion 3: Referral Program**
- **Offer:** Get 1 month free for each referral who becomes paying customer
- **Limit:** Unlimited
- **Goal:** Viral growth, reduce CAC
- **Expected Impact:** 10-20% of new customers from referrals

### Ongoing Discount Strategy

**Discount Policy:**
- **Never discount monthly pricing** (devalues product)
- **Only discount annual pricing** (aligns incentives)
- **Max discount: 20%** (preserves perceived value)
- **No custom discounts** (except for enterprise deals)

**When to Offer Discounts:**
- ✅ Annual contracts (20% off)
- ✅ Non-profit organizations (20% off)
- ✅ Long-term contracts (2+ years, 25% off)
- ❌ One-month discounts (creates expectation)
- ❌ "Just for you" discounts (hard to scale)
- ❌ Competitor matching (race to bottom)

**Enterprise Deal Structuring:**
- Start at $299/month base
- Add $50-100/month per additional 1,000 workflow runs
- Offer 20% discount for annual payment
- Offer 10% discount for 2-year commitment
- Include setup fee ($5,000) in first year

## 5. Pricing Psychology & Framing

### Value-Based Framing

**Don't Say:**
- "Our platform costs $99/month"

**Do Say:**
- "Save $400/month by consolidating 10 AI tools into 1 platform"
- "ROI within 60 days: Save 10 hours/week × $50/hour = $2,000/month value"
- "Replace your AI tool graveyard with one platform that just works"

### Price Anchoring

**Technique:** Show higher price first to make target price seem reasonable

**Example:**
- "Enterprise AI orchestration platforms cost $500-2,000/month"
- "We built the same features for small businesses"
- "Professional plan: $99/month (80% less than enterprise)"

### Decoy Effect

**Technique:** Add a "decoy" tier to make preferred tier seem like best value

**Example:**
- Starter: $49/month (5 workflows) ← Makes Professional seem expensive
- Professional: $99/month (25 workflows) ← Highlighted as "Most Popular"
- Enterprise: $299/month (unlimited) ← Makes Professional seem like bargain

**Result:** Most customers choose Professional (our highest-margin tier)

### Loss Aversion

**Technique:** Frame price as avoiding loss, not gaining benefit

**Example:**
- "Stop wasting $300/month on unused AI tools" (loss)
- vs. "Save $300/month with our platform" (gain)
- Loss framing 2x more effective (behavioral economics research)

## 7. Pricing Metrics & KPIs

### Key Pricing Metrics to Track

**Acquisition Metrics:**
- **Trial Signup Rate:** % of website visitors who start trial (target: 2-5%)
- **Trial-to-Paid Conversion:** % of trials that convert (target: 20-30%)
- **Average Revenue Per Customer (ARPC):** Total MRR / Total customers (target: $75+)
- **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** Total spend / New customers (target: $250-500)

**Revenue Metrics:**
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR):** Total subscription revenue per month
- **Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR):** MRR × 12
- **ARPC by Tier:** Starter ($49), Professional ($99), Enterprise ($299+)
- **Revenue Mix:** % Starter, % Professional, % Enterprise (target: 60/35/5)

**Pricing Efficiency Metrics:**
- **LTV:CAC Ratio:** Lifetime value / CAC (target: >5:1)
- **CAC Payback Period:** CAC / (ARPC × Gross Margin) (target: <12 months)
- **Gross Margin:** (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue (target: 60-70%)
- **Net Revenue Retention:** Revenue retention including upsells/churn (target: >100%)

**Behavioral Metrics:**
- **Price Objection Rate:** % of sales calls with price concerns (target: <30%)
- **Tier Upgrade Rate:** % of customers who upgrade tiers (target: 10-20%/year)
- **Annual vs. Monthly Split:** % customers on annual plans (target: 30-40%)
- **Discount Usage:** % customers with discounts (target: <20%)

### Pricing Review Cadence

**Monthly Reviews:**
- Trial-to-paid conversion rate
- ARPC trends
- Tier selection mix
- Price objections

**Quarterly Reviews:**
- Competitor pricing changes
- Customer willingness to pay (survey 20-30 customers)
- Pricing experiment results
- Adjust pricing if needed (±10-20%)

**Annual Reviews:**
- Comprehensive pricing analysis
- Market inflation adjustments
- Feature value reassessment
- Major pricing changes (new tiers, price increases)

## 9. Pricing Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Pricing Too High (Low Conversion)

**Signs:**
- Trial signup rate <2%
- Trial-to-paid conversion <15%
- >50% price objections in sales calls

**Mitigation:**
- Add lower tier ($29/month)
- Reduce prices by 10-20%
- Improve value communication (ROI calculator, case studies)
- Increase free trial to 30 days

### Risk 2: Pricing Too Low (Leaving Money on Table)

**Signs:**
- Trial-to-paid conversion >40%
- 0% price objections
- Most customers select highest tier

**Mitigation:**
- Increase prices by 10-20% across all tiers
- Add features to justify higher price
- Introduce premium tier above current highest
- Reduce discounts

### Risk 3: Pricing Misalignment (Wrong Customer Mix)

**Signs:**
- 80%+ customers in Starter tier (not upgrading)
- <5% customers in Enterprise tier
- ARPC <target

**Mitigation:**
- Add more features to Professional/Enterprise tiers
- Reduce Starter tier features (encourage upgrades)
- Improve upselling (in-app prompts, success team outreach)
- Introduce mid-tier between Starter and Professional

### Risk 4: Competitor Price War

**Signs:**
- Competitors lower prices
- Competitors launch "SMB" editions
- Customers mention competitor pricing

**Mitigation:**
- Don't match prices (race to bottom)
- Differentiate on value (support, templates, reliability)
- Add switching costs (templates, workflows, training)
- Focus on "you get what you pay for" messaging
