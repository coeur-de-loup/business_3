# Validation Stages

## Stage 1: Problem Validation

### Questions to Answer
- Do people actually have this problem?
- How painful is it? (nice-to-have vs must-have)
- Are they actively seeking solutions?
- How much would they pay to solve it?

### Validation Methods
| Method | Time | Cost | Confidence |
|--------|------|------|------------|
| Reddit/forum research | 2-4 hrs | Free | Medium |
| Competitor analysis | 2-4 hrs | Free | Medium |
| Search volume analysis | 1-2 hrs | Free | Low-Medium |
| Customer interviews | 1-2 weeks | Free | High |
| Survey (targeted) | 1 week | $50-200 | Medium-High |

### Pass Criteria
- [ ] Found 10+ people expressing this problem online
- [ ] Problem frequency: daily or weekly occurrence
- [ ] Current solutions have significant gaps
- [ ] People are already paying for alternatives

### Fail Triggers
- Can't find anyone discussing the problem
- Problem is solved well by existing solutions
- Problem is too niche (< 1000 potential customers)

---

## Stage 2: Solution Validation

### Questions to Answer
- Does our solution actually solve the problem?
- Is it better than alternatives?
- Can people understand the value quickly?
- What objections do they have?

### Validation Methods
| Method | Time | Cost | Confidence |
|--------|------|------|------------|
| Landing page test | 1 week | $50-100 | Medium |
| Prototype testing | 2 weeks | Free | High |
| Explainer video reactions | 3-5 days | $100-500 | Medium |
| Beta user feedback | 2-4 weeks | Free | High |

### Pass Criteria
- [ ] Landing page converts > 5% to signup
- [ ] Beta users complete core workflow
- [ ] NPS > 30 from beta users
- [ ] Users can explain value in their own words

### Fail Triggers
- Landing page converts < 1%
- Users abandon during onboarding
- Core features unused
- Value proposition doesn't resonate

---

## Stage 3: Business Model Validation

### Questions to Answer
- Will people pay the proposed price?
- What's the actual acquisition cost?
- What's the retention rate?
- Are unit economics viable?

### Validation Methods
| Method | Time | Cost | Confidence |
|--------|------|------|------------|
| Price testing A/B | 2 weeks | $200+ | High |
| Pre-orders | 2 weeks | Free | Very High |
| Paid pilot | 1 month | Free | Very High |
| Cohort analysis | 3 months | Free | Very High |

### Pass Criteria
- [ ] 10+ paying customers acquired
- [ ] CAC < 1/3 of LTV
- [ ] Monthly churn < 10%
- [ ] Positive unit economics

### Fail Triggers
- No one will pay
- CAC > LTV
- Churn > 20% monthly
- Requires unsustainable discounting

---

## Stage 4: Scale Validation

### Questions to Answer
- Can we acquire customers profitably at scale?
- Does the product work for diverse users?
- Can operations handle growth?
- What breaks at 10x volume?

### Validation Methods
| Method | Time | Cost | Confidence |
|--------|------|------|------------|
| Paid acquisition tests | 1 month | $1000+ | High |
| Geographic expansion | 2 months | Variable | High |
| Load testing | 1 week | Free | High |
| Support volume analysis | Ongoing | Free | Medium |

### Pass Criteria
- [ ] Profitable customer acquisition at scale
- [ ] Infrastructure handles 10x load
- [ ] Support processes documented
- [ ] Retention consistent across cohorts

### Fail Triggers
- CAC increases with scale
- Infrastructure fails under load
- Support becomes bottleneck
- Retention degrades with growth
